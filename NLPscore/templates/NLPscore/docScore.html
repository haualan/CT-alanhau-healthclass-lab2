Patient Engagement Tester


Websites such as Patients Like Me and Smart Patients have been instrumental in building social networks around patients and caregivers in the United States. These platforms have given an important outlet for providers, patients and their families to reach out and learn from each other outside the regulations and confinements of a hospital.

Over the years, these platforms have gathered bodies of texts or posts that patients ultimately find useful. Whether these posts are clinically useful remains unclear, however it is clear that readers typically upvote posts that emotionally resonate with them. Using these helpfulness votes and supervised machine learning techniques we can learn what patients feel strongly towards and better design models to classify helpfulness in unstructured text.

These models may support patient engagement and prove useful for providers and other healthcare content producers (such as WebMD, bloggers… etc) to create better reading materials for patients in the future.


This model specifically focuses on text that is related to Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)

Tips to improve patient engagement:
  • Use simple language with as little scientific jargon as possible
  • Use simple grammatical structures as if you are communicating casually with high-school level english
  • Describe both physical and emotional pain
  • Use narrative-like scenarios with a first-person point of view
